The Challenge: The "Screen Barrier" in Cultural Heritage

While digital technology has transformed how we access information, it has paradoxically distanced us from the physical world. In historical districts like the Shanghai Hengfu Area, visitors are often forced to choose between looking at the architecture or looking at a screen to read about it.

The Solution: An "Eyes-Free" Experience

Echoes is a wearable AR system powered by Multimodal Large Language Models (LLM). It eliminates the screen entirely, allowing users to explore history through a natural "Look & Ask" interaction model.

No Screens. No QRs. Just Look and Ask.

Technical Architecture: Powered by Multimodal AI

Echoes is built on a robust three-layer architecture designed to solve the challenges of latency and accuracy in real-time AR.

Perception Layer (StreamingVLM)

Utilizes GPT-4o Vision with a "StreamingVLM" framework to process continuous video feeds with low latency, ensuring the AI sees exactly what the user sees.

Cognition Layer (RAG & Vector DB)

Implements Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to ground AI responses in a verified historical database. This architectural choice critically mitigates "AI Hallucinations," ensuring historical accuracy while maintaining conversational fluidity.

Impact & Validation

By shifting the interface from "Touch & Read" to "Look & Listen," Echoes restores the "Genius Loci" (Spirit of Place). It empowers users like "Sarah" (a busy parent) to enjoy a hands-free tour while caring for her children, and allows photographers to capture moments without juggling devices.
