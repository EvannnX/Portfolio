Notification Center

Concept and design for the central communication channel with customers within the My Porsche App

Client
Porsche
Industry
Automotive
Services
Product Design, UX/UI Design, Testing
Year
2025

Case

Porsche made the strategic decision to replace the email-style inbox in its My Porsche app with a newly developed Notification Center, aiming to enhance the overall user experience and increase the service’s appeal.

Beyond the existing internal communications — invoices, bookings, purchases, and general updates — the new center also supports direct notifications from the vehicles themselves. Long-form emails were replaced by short, clear, and actionable messages.

Task

Define the concept and design, present a clear direction to engineering, and prepare a strategy for seamless integration into existing systems. I also helped teams previously relying on email communication transition to the new notification format.

Key Screens

Process & Components

Information Architecture

Prioritization distinguishes critical, actionable, and FYI signals. Clustering reduces noise while keeping pathways to detail.

Design System

Components with clear states (idle, pending, critical) and tokenized color roles ensure consistency across contexts.

Outcome

  • Decreased visual noise via contextual grouping and progressive detail.
  • Improved glanceability through type scale and contrast choices.
  • Systematic motion specs that communicate urgency without distraction.
